DO'S AND DON'Ts OF COVID-19

DO's DON'Ts
â€‹Wash your hands frequently (several times a day) for at least 20 seconds with soap and water. If you are in a situation where you can't avail soap and water, clean hands with an alcohol-based hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol. Make sure to clean your hand before eating or cooking, after sneezing or coughing, after touching a metal surface, after touching a sick person and after using the toilet. Don't touch your mouth, eyes, and nose if your hands are not washed. Unwashed hands might have virus that can enter your body when you touch the face.
Keep a check on the symptoms of coronavirus, which can range from common cough and fever to shortness of breath. Don't go outside of the home if you are not feeling well. If you are already traveling and get sick on the flight or cruise, inform the crew members immediately. Contact your doctor as soon as you get home.
Stay extra cautious at public places and if possible, avoid crowds and frequenting such places. Moreover, if you are over 60 years old or have a low risk tolerance, it is better to stay at home. Don't panic, according to public health officials the risk of coronavirus is still not that high. Prepare yourself and take proper precautions to save yourself from the attack. Not having or renewing your health insurance can be a big mistake.
If you are feeling sick, contact your doctor to know the procedure about what to do, instead of directly visiting the clinic, to avoid transmitting the infection to others.
